1. Event Overview

The Invitational is a multi-format, Ryder Cup–style golf competition played at Diamond Hills Country Club. Players compete in 9-hole matches using a variety of formats to earn points for their team.

While competitive, the event emphasizes:

  • Sportsmanship and fair play
  • Family participation and a welcoming atmosphere
  • Entertaining commentary and media coverage
  • Growing traditions and memorable moments

This rulebook applies to the 2026 edition and may be updated in future years as the event grows.

2. Teams & Captains

Team Structure

The field is divided into two teams with an equal number of players. Official permanent team names will be established after the inaugural event. Until then, teams are referred to as Team A and Team B in this rulebook.

Captains

Each team selects a Captain prior to the event. Captains are responsible for:

  • Selecting pairings and lineups for each session
  • Deciding when to use Advantage Tokens
  • Representing the team in any rules discussions or disputes
  • Helping establish team identity, including walk-up lines or future catchphrases

3. Match Format & Scoring

Formats Used

Across the event, the following 9-hole formats may be used:

  • Scramble
  • Best Ball
  • Alternate Shot
  • Singles Match Play

The specific schedule of formats and pairings will be determined by the event organizer and Captains.

Scoring System

  • Each 9-hole match is worth 1 point.
  • If a match is tied after 9 holes, each team earns ½ point.
  • Points from all matches and sessions are added together.
  • The team with the highest total points at the end of the event wins the Invitational.

Concessions

  • A putt, hole, or entire match may be conceded at any time.
  • Once something is conceded, it cannot be declined.

4. Pressure Holes System

Pressure Holes create high-stakes moments that reward clutch performance without creating runaway advantages. They are tied directly to Advantage Tokens that can be used later in the round.

Designated Pressure Holes (at Diamond Hills)

For 9-hole rounds played under this rulebook, the Pressure Holes are:

  • Hole 1
  • Hole 4
  • Hole 7

Pressure Hole Rewards

  • Winning Hole 1 → earns a Front Tee Token.
  • Winning Hole 4 → earns a Front Tee Token.
  • Winning Hole 7 → earns a Mulligan Token.

If a Pressure Hole is tied, no token is awarded for that hole.

5. Advantage Tokens

Advantage Tokens are strategic tools teams can earn by winning designated Pressure Holes. There are two types: the Front Tee Token and the Mulligan Token.

A. Front Tee Token

Earned On: Winning Pressure Hole 1 or 4.

Usage Rules

  • Must be used on the next non–par-3 hole after it is earned.
  • Cannot be used on a par 3.
  • Cannot be used on a Pressure Hole.
  • Must be used within the same 9-hole match.
  • Must be declared on the tee before either team hits.
  • Only one Front Tee Token may be used per round by a team.

Effect

When used, the team that earned the Token plays the designated hole from the forward tees. The opposing team plays from the standard tees. No additional strokes are added or removed.

Purpose

The Front Tee Token rewards clutch performance on Pressure Holes and creates exciting momentum swings early and mid-round without affecting the final hole.

B. Mulligan Token

Earned On: Winning Pressure Hole 7.

Usage Rules

  • Must be used before the end of the same 9-hole round.
  • May be used on any stroke except a putt on the green.
  • Must be declared before the next stroke is played.
  • Only one Mulligan Token may be used on a single hole.
  • Cannot be combined with a Front Tee Token on the same hole.

Effect

The player replays the chosen shot. The second shot must be used, even if it is worse than the original.

Purpose

The Mulligan Token provides a late-round strategic advantage, encourages aggressive play, and adds drama without altering teeing grounds on the closing holes.

6. Broadcast & Media Guidelines

On-course media coverage and commentary are core parts of the Invitational’s identity. The goal is to create a fun, engaging experience for players, families, and viewers.

Broadcast Crew

A basic broadcast crew consists of:

  • 2 Commentary Hosts (golfers or non-golfers; comedic commentary encouraged)
  • 1 Field Reporter
  • 1 Cameraman

Recommended 2026 Trial Equipment

  • Up to 4 GoPro-style cameras
  • Up to 4 lavalier microphones
  • Optional handheld camera for field shots
  • Audio recorders for commentary capture

Content Types

  • Pre-round introductions
  • On-course interviews and walk-and-talk segments
  • Desk-style live updates
  • Match recaps and post-round reactions
  • Lighthearted or comedic commentary, especially from non-golfers

7. Player Conduct & Pace of Play

Conduct Expectations

All participants are expected to:

  • Respect fellow players, volunteers, spectators, and media crew
  • Care for the course (repair divots, fix ball marks, rake bunkers)
  • Avoid dangerous or reckless behavior
  • Uphold the spirit of friendly competition

Pace of Play

The Invitational follows traditional honors-based play:

  • The side that wins a hole has the honor on the next tee.
  • The ball farthest from the hole plays first.
  • Groups may mutually agree to “ready golf” in certain situations, but honors remain the default.

8. Tiebreaker Procedures

If total points are tied at the end of all scheduled matches, the winner will be determined as follows:

Step 1 — Captains’ Putt-Off

Each Captain attempts a putt from a designated distance (for example, 20 feet). The closest ball to the hole wins. If both holed or both miss at the same distance, the organizer may repeat from a different location.

Step 2 — Sudden-Death Chip-Off

If still tied after the putt-off, each team selects one representative to hit a chip from a designated spot off the green. Closest to the hole wins.

Step 3 — Coin Flip

If still tied after both challenges, a simple coin flip between Captains will determine the winner for that year.

9. Awards & Traditions

Major Awards

  • Traveling Trophy (Cup) — awarded to the winning team’s Captain.
  • MVP — Most Valuable Player across the event.
  • Most Improved Player — notable improvement or development.
  • Clutch Performance Award — key shots or moments under pressure.
  • Best Teammate Award — sportsmanship, support, and attitude.

Optional Fun Awards

  • Funniest Shot or Moment
  • Best Interview or Quote
  • Best Walk-Up Music (future Party Hole feature)
  • Most Chaotic Cart Cam Clip

Awards may evolve as the event grows, but the goal is always to celebrate both performance and personality.

10. Future Expansion Roadmap (Non-Binding)

The following ideas are not part of the 2026 rules but serve as a long-term vision for how the Invitational may grow in future years.

A. Course & On-Course Enhancements

  • Designated “Party Hole” with seating, music, and live commentary.
  • Full tee-box signage kit showing formats, sponsors, and hole info.
  • Pin position sheets for each round.
  • Volunteer spotters and marshals on select holes.
  • Hole flyover videos integrated into the broadcast.

B. Broadcast & Media Upgrades

  • Drone coverage for intros, highlights, and key holes.
  • Dedicated broadcast desk with branded backdrop.
  • Replay system or basic shot-tracer effects.
  • Wireless mic packs for featured groups.
  • Additional field reporters and camera operators.
  • Live scoring app feeding directly into the broadcast and website.
  • Official highlight reel production for each year.

C. Website & Digital Expansion

  • Full player profile database with photos, bios, and stats.
  • Historical archive of past results, champions, and big moments.
  • Interactive brackets for a future Singles Invitational.
  • Enhanced broadcast hub page with integrated video and ticker.
  • Sponsor dashboard for logos, mentions, and hole partnerships.

D. Tournament Format Development

  • Large-field Singles Invitational (e.g., 64–128 players).
  • Official Par-3 Shootout with spectators and prizes.
  • Team Draft Night event, possibly live-streamed.
  • Skills Challenge Festival: long drive, flop wall, chipping ladder, putting gauntlet.
  • All-Star Challenge featuring past MVPs and notable personalities.

E. Equipment & Production Enhancements

  • Permanent cart-mounted camera rigs with power solutions.
  • Centralized lav mic rack and charging station.
  • DJ/PA system for Party Hole and ceremonies.
  • Professional lighting and backdrop for interviews and awards.
  • Earpieces and simple comms for the broadcast team.

F. Event Logistics & Experience

  • Opening Ceremonies with player introductions and Captain speeches.
  • Closing Ceremonies with recap montage and full awards presentation.
  • Family Fan Zone with lawn games and viewing areas.
  • Merchandise (hats, shirts, pins, posters, scorecards).
  • Volunteer program for scoring, spotting, and media support.

G. Administration & Governance

  • Permanent team names formally adopted after the inaugural event.
  • Annual Captains’ Meeting to review formats and rules.
  • Small Rules Committee to handle disputes.
  • Statistical tracking system for players and teams.
  • Qualification or invitation criteria as the field grows.
